Lesson 2: Wired and Wireless Mice

Students will be able to differentiate between a wired and a wireless computer mouse.

Students will be able to connect a wired and a wireless computer mouse to a desktop computer.


Video Lesson



Wired Mice

A wired mouse connects directly to your computer, usually through a USB port (see bottom figure), and transmits information via the attached cord. Note the cord at the top of the figure above. The cord connection can sometimes provide advantages versus a cordless mouse. For starters, wired mice can provide faster response times, with data transmitted directly through the cable. They also tend to be more accurate than other designs.

Wireless Mice

Wireless mice transmit signals to a receiver that is connected to your computer via a USB port. The computer receives the signals and interprets how the cursor was moved or which buttons on the mouse were clicked. Wireless mice provide easier movement and a larger range to use the mouse, however there are some drawbacks. Wireless mice are generally not as responsive to certain gestures as are wired mice. For most users, however, the lag or loss of accuracy with a wireless mouse will not be an issue.
